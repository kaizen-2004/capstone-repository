# Raspberry Pi - Foreign Studies

Kadhim et al. (2023) created a wearable system for people with Alzheimer's that used an ESP32-CAM to record video and a Raspberry Pi to recognize faces and give audio feedback. The prototype had a high accuracy rate of about 99% and a low latency rate of about 1 second. This shows that low-power embedded devices can handle real-time image processing. This study shows how ESP32 is better for lightweight sensing and streaming and Raspberry Pi is better for edge computation. This sets a good example for how to combine the two devices. It is directly relevant to this study because it supports a separated-role architecture in which distributed ESP32 capture/sensing is paired with dedicated hosts for backend and vision processing. This information helps shape the current intruder and fire alert implementation, where Raspberry Pi 2 handles server-side services and a second-hand laptop handles face/fire inference. Real-time public safety has been a focus for decentralized monitoring and quick responses to events. An IoT-based emergency-response system was created in one study. It had environmental and biometric sensors that were linked to edge computing devices like Raspberry Pi and ESP32. The time it took for the system to intervene in this study was less than 450 milliseconds, and the accuracy of the detection was over 95% for fire, traffic, gas leak, and medical emergencies. The system also reported a 99.1 percent success rate for delivering alerts and a 99.8 percent uptime for more than 12,000 devices.
These findings demonstrate the viability of Raspberry Pi and ESP32 in urban and industrial environments for a large-scale emergency monitoring system (Zhang et al., 2025). The paper on home intrusion detection looked into how ESP32 could be used in a safety app for regular people. The prototype was made up of a PIR motion sensor, a magnetic door switch, a buzzer, an LED light, and a camera. Using GSM modules, SMS alerts were made and sent. This made it possible to monitor and control things from a distance. In terms of functionality, tests showed that the system could find unauthorized entries and send alerts at the same time.
Foreign research demonstrates the concept of IoT-based public safety systems employing distributed sensors and edge computing for the swift detection of emergencies. In a real-time system, fire, gas, vibration, and biometric sensing were merged, and Raspberry Pi and ESP32 were used for on-site data handling. The sub-450 ms alert time, detection accuracy of more than 95 percent, and operational stability of over 12,000 connected devices were the performance metrics of the system. These results demonstrate that Raspberry Pi and ESP32 can facilitate large-scale emergency monitoring reliably (Zhang et al., 2025).
