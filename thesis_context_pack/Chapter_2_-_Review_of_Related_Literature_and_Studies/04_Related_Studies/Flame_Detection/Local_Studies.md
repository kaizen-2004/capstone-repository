# Flame Detection - Local Studies

Local fire detection research reflects a similar progression, beginning with threshold-based multi-sensor systems designed to reduce false alarms. Studies such as Sansolis et al. (2021), Molero et al. (2025), and Bantaculo et al. (2024) integrated gas, temperature, and flame sensors using simple rule-based logic. These systems were cost-effective and widely accessible but relied on static decision rules that could not adapt to complex or evolving fire scenarios.
To address this limitation, adaptive intelligence was introduced. Maquiran et al. (2025) enhanced multi-sensor systems with artificial intelligence, reporting reduced false alarms and improved detection accuracy compared to fixed-threshold designs. In contrast, Calo and Barbosa (2024) proposed a video-based fire detection system using machine learning classifiers, achieving very high accuracy. However, the system required powerful computing hardware, making it unsuitable for large-scale or low-cost residential deployment.
