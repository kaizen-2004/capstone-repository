# Flame Detection - Foreign Studies

Recent foreign research on flame detection has shifted toward computer vision and deep learning approaches. Prema et al. (2017) utilized texture-based volumetric descriptors to model flame motion, demonstrating the potential of handcrafted visual features. However, these methods remained sensitive to lighting, camera angle, and scene variability, limiting their robustness in real-world conditions (Ma et al., 2020).
More recently, deep learning approaches have dominated the field. Ryu and Kwak (2021) employed convolutional neural networks to achieve high accuracy in diverse scenarios, highlighting the effectiveness of data-driven models over handcrafted features. Despite this, the high computational demands of deep networks restrict their use in low-power or real-time embedded systems. Overall, foreign studies emphasize a recurring trade-off between detection accuracy and system efficiency, pointing to the need for models that are both reliable and computationally practical.
