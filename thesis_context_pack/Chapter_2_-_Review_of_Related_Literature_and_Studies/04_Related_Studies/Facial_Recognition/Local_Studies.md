# Facial Recognition - Local Studies

Local researchers point out facial recognition as an effective tool for identification and surveillance in the Philippines. One of the pieces of research introduced PCA- and LBPH-based systems as means of safe access by using facial features to confirm the identities of those already registered in controlled areas (Batinggal & Niguidula, 2018). Another local project turned to Raspberry Pi combined with LBP and LBPH for the purposes of attendance recording and offline identification of people. These devices are capable of delivering excellent results in controlled settings and they come at a price that is affordable for community-level surveillance (Virata & Festijo, 2022). An investigative report on the use of open-source facial recognition in law enforcement for the identification of criminals was also produced. Algorithms were challenged with different illumination and pose conditions, and as a result, the study pointed to the function of oversight as a prerequisite when recognition is employed in investigations (Rey et al., 2022).
Among other things, deep learning techniques that can identify faces even if they are masked were considered, too. The said technologies comprise health-screening components and thermal sensors, thereby achieving real-time attendance logging and at the same time, ID verification even when the situation is health-oriented (Bautista et al., 2023). Lastly, a project on motion-triggered logbook systems employing PCA and LBPH for the creation of a facility was realized. A particular face can be grabbed by the apparatus only when it detects the occurrence of an event; hence identification is done by means of light hardware automatically (Maramba et al., 2018).
