# Facial Recognition - Foreign Studies

Research abroad reveals that facial recognition is the leading technology in the security and surveillance systems of the future. Scientists have created IoT-based security platforms that integrate PIR sensors, cameras, and real-time face recognition to secure homes and office spaces. These devices execute identity confirmation without human intervention, thus, they increase the response rate and cut down on the manual monitoring required (Bagchi et al., 2022). Meanwhile, some works implement facial recognition for limited access in cars and buildings only. By utilizing certain algorithms like Haar cascades and LBPH, these installations instantly obtain the user's face, authenticate, and, according to the result, perform or refuse access thus demonstrating, biometrics as more and more the choice of secure authentication (Elngar & Kayed, 2020).
Moreover, implant surveillance studies highlight the employment of the Raspberry Pi platform in the creation of minimal-expense facial recognition systems. Such low-cost systems are equipped to handle identification and detection of persons in off-limit locations, facilitate the logging of recognized faces, besides being able to provide up-to-date surveillance even with restricted hardware resources (Kaundanya et al., 2017). Another part of the investigation introduces the use of robots fitted with cameras and sensors to perform face identification in real-time. The operations are carried out by Raspberry Pi-based devices that locally process the images, identify the intruder and send notifications; hence, they are quite helpful in conditions of poor light or for indoor surveillance (Meddeb et al., 2022). The latest piece of work implies a combination of CNN models and YOLO with the purpose of locating faces right from the edge devices. Conducting recognition locally shortens the waiting time, enhances privacy, and thus, making it possible for quick checking to be done in the case of smart towns and IoT (Shanker et al., 2024).
