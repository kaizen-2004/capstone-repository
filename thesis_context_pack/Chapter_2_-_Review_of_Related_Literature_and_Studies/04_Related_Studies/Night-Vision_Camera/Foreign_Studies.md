# Night-Vision Camera - Foreign Studies

Research from other countries shows that night-vision enhancement has many direct uses. Li, Luo, et al. (2024) conducted research on target image classification under low-light night-vision conditions. Cho et al. (2018) showed that it was possible to find faces at night using visible-light camera sensors and a two-step Faster R-CNN method. Chien et al. (2017) devised classification methodologies to enhance nighttime driving safety via vision-based recognition.
Studies on improving images also support night-vision systems. Shi et al. (2018) proposed a single-image enhancement method utilizing bright/dark channel priors, whereas Ai et al. (2020) employed an attention-based U-net architecture for extreme low-light surveillance. Zhou et al. (2024) added real-time low-light enhancement to smartphones, and Manssor et al. (2022) used thermal infrared imaging to find people at night.
Bai et al. (2023) proposed a bio-inspired method that combines contrast enhancement and noise reduction, and Zhang et al. (2020) developed an attention-based network for improving low-light images. A practical illustration is the research conducted by V. SB et al. (2023), which developed a night-vision firefighter robot, demonstrating the utility of night-vision imaging in rescue operations.
