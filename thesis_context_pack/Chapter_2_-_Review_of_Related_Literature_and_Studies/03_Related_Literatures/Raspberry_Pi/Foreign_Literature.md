# Raspberry Pi - Foreign Literature

Research on the communication and coordination of Raspberry Pi and ESP32 devices has expanded as IoT systems continue to require cost effective, scalable, and resilient architectures. The IEEE study on an MQTT based monitoring system for urban farming demonstrated early feasibility of combining ESP32 sensors with a Raspberry Pi gateway for distributed environmental observation. While the system effectively collected and transmitted data, it depended entirely on Wi-Fi and cloud brokers, limiting its reliability in unstable network conditions. Further evidence of device capability differences appears in Malović et al. (2024), who compared the performance of Raspberry Pi and ESP32 CAM modules for image processing tasks. Their findings show that the Raspberry Pi consistently maintains higher frame rates and detection accuracy under increased computational loads, whereas the ESP32 CAM struggles with complex inputs and fails under high face count scenarios. This comparison highlights the importance of selecting appropriate hardware for processing intensive tasks while managing cost constraints.
Standards work provides another perspective. ISO 7240 13 outlines guidelines for fire detection and alarm communication structures, emphasizing reliability, interoperability, and structured signaling in safety-oriented systems. Although focused on fire detection, the principles of dependable data transfer and fault tolerant communication are relevant to IoT designs using Raspberry Pi and ESP32. Complementing these formal studies, Peregrino’s Medium articles present practical implementations of direct BLE communication between Raspberry Pi and ESP32, showcasing low latency, cloud free device interaction suitable for lightweight edge networks. Collectively, these works reveal a shift from cloud dependent architectures toward more autonomous and efficient device level communication models.
Experiments with automated surveillance suggest that Raspberry Pi can handle local human detection that doesn't require cloud support. The study chose HOG and SVM as the most efficient methods for real-time operations in low-power hardware and reported that the performance of the Raspberry-Pi-based systems in terms of accuracy remains stable when using a single benchmark dataset (Khalifa et al., 2019).
To enable Bluetooth Low Energy connection between a Raspberry Pi as the main device and an ESP32 as a peripheral, Peregrino (2025) provides a detailed, step-by-step instruction. Before demonstrating how the Pi receives temperature data sent by the ESP32 and how the ESP32 blinks an onboard LED to signal connection status, he goes over the hardware, necessary Python and MicroPython libraries, and programming tools.
