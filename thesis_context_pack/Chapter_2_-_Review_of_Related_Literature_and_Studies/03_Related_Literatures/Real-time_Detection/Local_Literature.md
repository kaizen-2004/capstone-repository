# Real-time Detection - Local Literature

In succession to developments of sophisticated algorithms for face recognition, other research highlights the significance of real-time, smart surveillance systems. Today’s CCTV systems has already integrate a high-resolution cameras, cloud-edge computing, and deep learning analytics to effectively monitor unusual activities,detect objects, and deliver actionable alerts (Suniway, 2025; Flatworld Solutions Philippines, 2025; Lee 2025). By reducing response times, improving situational awareness, and having predictive capabilities, these systems have shown enhanced overall security. The combination of hardware and software, and the acceleration Graphical Processing Unit (GPU) and Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGA), is important to achieve the necessary performance for practical standard for deployment of real-time systems.
The literature shows that it is evident in disaster response systems that the unification of IoT and AI was feasible in emergency management. Connecting sensors such as smoke alarms and flood detectors was demonstrated by Wiredfield Philippines, Inc.’s Alerto PH, this enables AI-driven alert mechanisms, which allows fast response in high-risk environments (Daily Tribune, 2016). Combining sensor data, AI analytics, and mobile notifications shown by these systems justify the current research for its feasibility and helping the community to improve resilience and safety.
This study addresses the identified gap by combining real-time facial recognition with multi-criteria fire detection to reduce false alarms in condo-like settings. The implementation leverages a split-host architecture: Raspberry Pi 2 for local server hosting (dashboard/API/database/alerts), a second-hand laptop for face/fire vision processing, plus ESP32 devices, night-vision imaging, and optical smoke sensing in a unified pipeline. For node-to-server communication, the implemented protocol is HTTP POST + JSON rather than MQTT. The proposed framework aims to provide proactive security and emergency alerts through an integrated, practical, and responsive design.
