# Introduction

New automated security and safety systems have been developed by integrating the Internet of Things (IoT), computer vision, and sensing technologies. Some of the earlier works talk about facial recognition based on the IoT that has been implemented on ultra-low-cost platforms like Raspberry Pi and ESP32 for identity verification in smart environments (Irjanto & Surantha, 2020). Meanwhile, another concurrent article presents the fire detection system based on the IoT with smoke sensors such as MQ-2 and the MQTT protocol for real-time transmission and early warning, which was carried out (Morchid et al., 2024).The most recent study partly supports the systems that are combined through facial recognition, fire detection, and wireless alerting that, besides reliability, response time can be improved (Sruthy et al., 2020). This paper is mainly focusing on real-time monitoring with reliable low-light performance via night-vision imaging and therefore it is a contribution to the above-mentioned research area.
The review of the literature indicates that the integration of facial recognition, fire detection, and IoT alerting within the same platforms has been successful. The systems work with the Raspberry Pi or ESP32 to perform OpenCV-based face recognition and at the same time use smoke sensing with alert latencies of fewer than five seconds (Gu et al., 2022a; Zoha et al., 2025a). On edge devices, MobileNet, a small model, identifies faces with an accuracy of more than 98 percent, whereas multi-sensor configurations enable the detection of various threats at the same time with high effectiveness (Mandala et al., 2023a). However, there are still limitations. Performance leaves recognition under low light, continuous vision processing consumes too much power, and the fusion of heterogeneous sensor data is a bit weak for stable operation (Rahim et al., 2023a). Therefore, illumination-robust real-time performance is still a challenge.
Security systems at present are mostly reactive. They will only show you the recorded evidence after the crime but will not help you in crime prevention or early response. One report from the industry basically says that standard security cameras are mostly just passive recording instruments with a few proactive features (Safewise). Intrusion detection and fire monitoring are the two separate systems most of the time, which results in fragmented monitoring, delayed alerts, and more work for the user. Motion-based detection keeps on generating false alarms because it can’t tell a user from an intruder. Cameras at a low price point also end up being a bit inaccurate in low light situations. Many systems also do not have integrated real-time mobile notifications and offer limited user control for identity management and activity review.
The main focus of this work is to develop and deploy a real-time intruder and fire monitoring alert system through IoT-based facial recognition and night-vision imaging. The current implementation covers two monitored areas (Living Room and Door Entrance Area), using an indoor USB UVC camera and an outdoor ESP32-CAM stream over HTTP. Sensor nodes submit smoke and door-force events to the server through HTTP POST + JSON via `POST /api/sensors/event` (not MQTT in the implementation). The current deployment uses a split-host architecture: Raspberry Pi 2 hosts the local server stack (dashboard, API, database, and alerts), while a second-hand laptop runs face-recognition and visual flame-detection processing. Facial database handling and sensor/event logs are managed through a Python Flask web interface. The system's recognition performance, fire false alarm rate, and end-to-end latency under different light conditions are metrics used to assess performance.
Keeping statistics at a national level clearly shows that there is a definite need for safety systems that are proactive. Fire incident cases recorded by the Bureau of Fire Protection throughout the year from January to December 2024 reached a total of 18,217 incidents or 11.2 percent more than the previous year which saw 338 civilian deaths and property losses worth ₱13.8 billion (Bureau of Fire Protection, 2024). On the other hand, during the same period, crimes against properties went down by 66.81 percent, as per the report of the Philippine National Police (2024). Such a decrease signals the need for strengthening preventive security measures. A fire and security emergencies rise culmination justifies a real-time integrated solution for safety. The proposed system would be a response to this issue through its integrated intrusion and fire detection with the feature of alerting that is instant aimed at response time being further shortened.
