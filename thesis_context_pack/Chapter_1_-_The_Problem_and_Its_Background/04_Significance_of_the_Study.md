# Significance of the Study

The proposed system is significant to residents because it offers a proactive local monitoring approach rather than a purely passive recording setup. It is intended to recognize suspicious presence near the monitored entrance, respond to smoke-related conditions with visual confirmation support, and preserve logs and alerts that help the user review important incidents. By combining multiple sources of evidence, the system seeks to reduce false alarms and provide more meaningful event classification than standalone motion or smoke alarms.

The study is also significant to researchers and developers because it demonstrates how facial recognition, smoke sensing, door-force sensing, and fire-related visual analysis can be combined in a single IoT-based monitoring workflow. It provides a practical reference for event-driven fusion logic, local alert management, and low-light monitoring in condo-like settings using accessible hardware and software tools. The inclusion of Raspberry Pi 2 as an optional local edge-hosting component also keeps the work relevant to embedded-system deployment studies.

Finally, the results of this study may serve as a basis for future work on smart residential monitoring, especially in areas such as alert reliability, response time, local-first operation, and scalable deployment of integrated safety and security systems.
