# Significance of the Study

The proposed system stands to add value in numerous ways. First and foremost, this system will give residents a single monitoring device that is proactive and can recognize suspicious individuals at the door; it can also detect first signs of fire dangers and allow the residents to go through a short record of major unit events. Unlike traditional systems that are only capable of capturing footage or setting off a lone smoke alarm, this prototype focuses on smart detection and classification and thus can limit the number of false alarms by necessitating the approval of several sensors for a fire confirmation.
In terms of technology and academics, the paper is a proof of concept for multi-sensor fusion, the integration of facial recognition, visual flame detection, smoke sensing, and door motion analysis in a single edge device with low-cost components. The results of detection efficiency, false alarm rate, and reaction time can be a useful reference for further studies not only in the development of safety systems at the condo-scale but also in the practical constraints of computer vision and sensing tasks on embedded platforms.
