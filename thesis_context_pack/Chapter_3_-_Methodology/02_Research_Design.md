# Research Design

The present study embraced a developmental and experimental research design. It was developmental in nature because it dealt with the design and construction of a real-time intruder and fire monitoring alert system equipped with facial recognition, fire-related visual analysis, distributed smoke sensing, and door-force sensing technologies. The work also had an experimental character because the completed prototype was subjected to controlled tests under different scenarios such as normal activity, intruder-related conditions, fire-related conditions, and nuisance conditions in order to record its operational behavior.
The method employed one or more phases of the engineering process:
system context, scope, and architecture design;
development of core detection modules (face, flame, smoke, door-force);
system integration and multi-sensor fusion;
iterative testing and refinement; and
final performance evaluation.
On the conclusion of each phase, there are specific deliverables (validated modules, integrated prototype, refined configuration, and performance metrics) that are a direct response to the research objectives.


**Implementation note:** Raspberry Pi 2 is retained as an optional deployment component for local edge hosting. The methodology evaluates the integrated system by function and behavior rather than by one permanently fixed host arrangement.
