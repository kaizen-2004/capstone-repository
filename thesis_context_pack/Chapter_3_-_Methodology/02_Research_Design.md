# Research Design

The present study embraced a developmental and experimental research design. It was developmental in nature as it dealt with the design and building of a split-host real-time intruder and fire monitoring alert system equipped with facial recognition, visual flame detection, distributed smoke sensing, and door-force sensing technologies. The work also had an experimental character as the prototype, once completed, is put through controlled tests under different scenarios (normal activity, intruder, fire-related, and nuisance conditions) to be able to record its operational efficiency.
The method employed one or more phases of the engineering process:
system context, scope, and architecture design;
development of core detection modules (face, flame, smoke, door-force);
system integration and multi-sensor fusion;
iterative testing and refinement; and
final performance evaluation.
On the conclusion of each phase, there are specific deliverables (validated modules, integrated prototype, refined configuration, and performance metrics) that are a direct response to the research objectives.


**Implementation note (revision):** Raspberry Pi 2 is used for local server hosting (dashboard/API/database/alerts), while a second-hand laptop is used to run face-recognition and visual flame-detection processing.
