# Overview

This chapter details the approach utilized by the researchers in the design, development, and performance testing of a real-time intruder and fire monitoring alert system for condominium-like spaces. The methodology primarily reflects developmental and experimental research and is guided by computer vision, sensor fusion, local monitoring, and event-driven system design. The prototype includes camera-based analysis, smoke sensing, door-force sensing, alert management, and persistent local logging. Raspberry Pi 2 is treated in this methodology as an optional deployment component for local edge hosting rather than a mandatory fixed host role.

The chapter is organized into five phases: (1) system context, scope, and architecture design; (2) development of core detection modules; (3) system integration and multi-sensor intelligence layer; (4) iterative testing, adjustment, and refinement; and (5) final performance evaluation and analysis.
The chapter also provides an account of the research design, research setting, respondents, instruments, procedures per phase, and data analysis techniques employed to gauge the system’s performance.
