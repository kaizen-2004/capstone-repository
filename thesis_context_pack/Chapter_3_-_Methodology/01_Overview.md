# Overview

This chapter details the approach utilized by the researchers in the design, development, and performance testing of a split-host real-time intruder and fire monitoring alert system specifically for condominium units. The methodology primarily reflects developmental/engineering research and is enhanced by the supporting concepts figuring computer vision, sensor fusion, and pattern recognition. In the current setup, Raspberry Pi 2 is used for server hosting (dashboard/API/database/alerts), while a second-hand laptop is used for face-recognition and flame-detection processing. It has been segregated into five phases: (1) system context, scope, and architecture design; (2) development of core detection modules; (3) system integration and multi-sensor intelligence layer; (4) iterative testing, adjustment, and refinement; and (5) final performance evaluation and analysis.
The chapter also provides an account of the research design, research setting, respondents, instruments, procedures per phase, and data analysis techniques employed to gauge the system’s performance.
